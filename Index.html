My Life Story

Hello everybody, it's me, DJS_YT/Debasis. I am going to write about my life story. Let's begin.

So, it started when I was around 8 years old. My class 2 almost ended, then my father decided to put me in a hostel. He also admitted me to class 2 again, so I studied class 2 for the second time. After a year, COVID-19 came, and all schools were closed.

During that time, I got to know about smartphones for the first time and learned so much about them. I was the mobile genius in my house, and I still am. After about two years, lockdown ended, and schools reopened. My father put me in another hostel, where I was so bullied by other children.

After a year, during summer vacation, my family decided to go on a trip to Puri. We all got ready—me, my brother, my father, my mother, my uncle, another uncle who was driving, and my aunt. We left home around 5 PM. I fell asleep at 7 PM and woke up at around 1 AM or 2 AM for the toilet. That’s when I saw someone...

I told my father about it, but he said he couldn't see anything. However, I could see someone just staring at me, not moving. I was so scared. We started the car again, and I went back to sleep. When I woke up, we had reached Puri.

We visited the Puri Jagannath Temple. At the temple gate, there are some steps, and you should never step on the 3rd one. But I did. I didn't know at the time that if you step on it, all the good deeds you've done in life are erased.

After that, we went to Puri Beach. Then we decided to visit my uncle's house in Cuttack. By the time we reached there, the sun had already set.

Later, at around 12:45 AM, something bad happened. My uncle, who was driving, lost control, and the car hit a divider, flew over the bridge, and crashed. A few minutes later, a truck driver saw us and called an ambulance.

The ambulance took us to the hospital. When I woke up, I was in the hospital. My father was in serious condition, so they transferred him to another hospital in Rourkela. I didn't mention earlier that my leg was broken in the accident, but at the hospital, they treated it, and now it's fine.

That's my story. Bye!
